Side Project 2025 Health & Wellness Vibe Coded Launching Soon
Day One Again
A non-judgemental platform for cannabis users to track their usage, understand their patterns, and reconnect with themselves — built by someone who lived the problem.
Status
Launching in ~2 weeks
Built with
ChatGPT · Claude · Lovable
My role
Founder · Designer · Builder
The Problem

For three years, I used cannabis heavily. What started as recreational slowly became a crutch — and I didn't notice until the signs were impossible to ignore: lower motivation, emotional flatness, a growing detachment from people I cared about and things that once mattered to me.

I had good friends around me. A support system most people don't have. And even then, quitting was hard. Not just physically — but emotionally. Every bad day felt like a setback. Every slip felt like failure. There was no space to process it without judgement, no tool that understood the nuance of what I was going through.

"If it was this hard for me — with support, with awareness, with the ability to use AI tools to help myself — what does it feel like for someone doing this completely alone?"
The Insight

The turning point wasn't a single moment. It was a realisation that built slowly: there are people out there who are alone in this. Who don't have friends to lean on, who don't know how to use ChatGPT to their advantage, who are silently struggling with something society still treats with stigma.

Most existing apps are either clinical and cold, or built for abstinence-only thinking. They don't account for the messy, non-linear reality of trying to change a habit that has emotional roots. They don't make you feel seen.

Day One Again is built on one belief: that every single day is a fresh start. That "day one again" isn't a sign of failure — it's proof you're still trying. And that trying, consistently, is what progress actually looks like.

What It Does

Day One Again gives cannabis users a private, non-judgemental space to:

Track usage and recovery trends — so users can see their own patterns clearly, without shame. Data as self-awareness, not self-punishment.

Connect with society on their own terms — for those who are ready, a gentle on-ramp back to community and connection, not forced or rushed.

Mark every day one as meaningful — the name isn't ironic. It's a reframe. Whether it's your first day one or your fiftieth, it counts. The app is designed to make you feel that.

How I Built It — As a Non-Tech PM

I'm a Product Manager. I know how to think about products. But writing code from scratch? That's not my background. What changed is the world around me.

I used ChatGPT to think through structure and content, Claude to refine the experience and writing, and Lovable to bring the interface to life — going from idea to working product without a single line of code written by hand.

I expected it to be overwhelming. It wasn't. What surprised me most was how much product thinking still mattered — knowing what to ask, how to frame the problem, when to push back on what the AI generated. The tools handled the execution. The judgement was still mine.

Why It Matters to Me

I'm building this because I know what it feels like to not be able to quit for a long time. To feel stuck. To want things to be different but not know where to start.

I got through it. Not perfectly, not linearly. But I got through it. And the least I can do is build something that gives someone else — someone who might be more alone in this than I was — a place to start their own day one.
